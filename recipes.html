Beena’s Kitchen – Kerala Recipes

1. Kerala Sambar

A traditional lentil-based vegetable stew made with toor dal, tamarind, and a mix of vegetables.

Calories: 180 | Carbs: 24g | Protein: 8g | Fat: 4g

Health Tip: High in fiber and protein. Good for diabetics and heart health.

2. Aviyal

A coconut and curd-based semi-dry dish made with mixed vegetables and coconut oil.

Calories: 150 | Carbs: 18g | Protein: 5g | Fat: 6g

Health Tip: Gluten-free and rich in antioxidants.

3. Beetroot Pachadi

Sweet and tangy beetroot dish with coconut and yogurt. Served during Sadya.

Calories: 120 | Carbs: 16g | Protein: 3g | Fat: 5g

Health Tip: Beetroot supports blood pressure and heart health.

4. Kadala Curry

Black chickpea curry with roasted coconut gravy. Often served with puttu.

Calories: 210 | Carbs: 30g | Protein: 10g | Fat: 7g

Health Tip: High protein and fiber. Great for sugar control.

5. Ulli Kozhi (Shallot Chicken)

Spicy chicken curry cooked with crushed shallots, garlic, and pepper.

Calories: 250 | Carbs: 8g | Protein: 18g | Fat: 14g

Health Tip: Boosts immunity. Avoid excess oil for heart safety.

6. Coconut Arachu Chicken Curry

Rich Kerala chicken curry made with fresh ground coconut and spices.

Calories: 270 | Carbs: 10g | Protein: 20g | Fat: 16g

Health Tip: Good protein source. Use less oil for cholesterol care.

7. Kudampuli Itta Meen Curry

Sour and spicy fish curry made with kudampuli and coconut oil. Signature Kerala flavor.

Calories: 200 | Carbs: 4g | Protein: 22g | Fat: 10g

Health Tip: Omega-3 rich. Good for heart and skin.

8. Coconut Chutney

Simple and fresh chutney made with grated coconut, chilies, and tempered mustard seeds.

Calories: 90 | Carbs: 4g | Protein: 2g | Fat: 8g

Health Tip: Low carb, natural fat. Ideal side for dosas and idlis.
